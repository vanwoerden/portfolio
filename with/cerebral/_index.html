Related experience in recruitment

From late 2017 to early 2020 I led product design for Top20Talent, a recruitment platform. If you think about it, there are some similarities between recruitment and healthcare:

  • rigid protocols and workflows,
  • healthcare professionals (and HR) being forced to work with non-optimal tools and tech,
  • low visibility into the process for patients (and jobseekers),
  • the incentives for insurers/big pharma (and recruiters) to cut costs/make a profit,
  • “digital transformations” that don't seem to take hold...

...just to name a few. Building solutions that somehow reconcile all of these opposing forces and misaligned incentives is hard. But it's also what makes design so interesting.

A desire to learn

I've always been drawn to fiction and stories. I love how Neil Gaiman puts it: Fiction uses a lie [a wolf pretends to be a grandmother] to tell you the truth [stranger danger].

I went remote in January 2020, like many others, and solid written communication suddenly became essential.

So now I'm reading Stephen King's On Writing, and watching Neil Gaiman and James Patterson on Masterclass to learn how to write better. I recently wrote about how horror and usability testing are eerily similar.

I like picking up new skills and tools. When I wanted to figure out whether the air quality in Beijing was improving over the long term, I learned just enough about D3.js to build a visualization (work in progress, not mobile friendly) that explores the city's air quality data.

  • For the past four years, I've played the part of sole designer and product manager. That means I work on a lot of different things. In a principal role I can focus on the craft and have a fair amount of strategic impact. Very appealing.

  • Making a difference in peoples' lives gets me excited. It was a key reason for me to join Our World in Data, and Gather Health before that.
  • Remote comes with its own challenges, but on the whole it's a selling point for me.
