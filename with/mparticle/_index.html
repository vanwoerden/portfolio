Ernst & mParticle?

This page outlines why I'm interested in joining the mParticle design team, and highlights some of my previous work.

About me

I'm Ernst, a Dutch designer based in New York (LI). Over the past 11 years, I've worked in mostly in product, user experience, and interaction design, but also in branding, motion design and animation. I've spent the majority of my time in-house at startups.

I've explored working for myself, in agencies, and big tech in China, and I've found I'm happiest when I work on the product side of design, in highly collaborative organizations.

For designers to have true impact, end-to-end involvement in the product development process is crucial. I was excited to hear that product and design seem to be on equal footing at mParticle. I'm very curious to learn in detail how your product development process works, and how engineering, users, and other stakeholders are involved.

I thrive in collaborative environments where we discover the best solution together, or discover where we’re wrong early on.

I currently lead design for Our World in Data, a website that makes research and data on the world's biggest problems accessible, understandable, and usable. We work very incrementally, and my role as the only designer/product person encompasses user research, scoping features, lo-fi and hi-fi design and prototyping, usability testing, shaping the roadmap, guiding long-term strategy, and evaluating product performance.

It’s a great team of smart, dedicated, and thoughtful people, and the work is meaningful. But what I’m missing, and what I think mParticle could offer, is a team with more design peers and veterans.

A team in which I could spread myself a little less thin, while still having a fair amount of product and strategic impact through design. A place where I could learn from, collaborate with, and help other designers.

Highlighted work

A few examples of relevant past work:

Decreasing the distance to data

The first stage of moving a few key numbers to the top of the covid-19 vaccinations page. User research findings and a quick comparison with Google's search results page made the problem clear and the first step towards a solution straightforward, and data about returning users showed that the timing was right to execute on a related idea.

AQI.fyi

A personal project where I explore if and how much the air quality in Beijing is improving. The weather in Beijing is quite predictable. The air quality is what determines whether it's a nice day for the pool or a sad Saturday spent inside. There are good and bad months throughout the year, but it's hard to tell if things are actually improving or not without looking at the data.

Users and change—the Δ in design

A case study about how the “projects” feature at Top20Talent evolved over the course of ~1.5 years. There's a good amount of detail in there. Looking back, I have some doubts about the direction we took towards the end.

The horror of usability testing

A piece I wrote about how designers should take a page from the horror movie genre. Observing a usability test is remarkably similar to watching a horror movie: it’s a chance for things to take a turn, from the safety of the ‘lab’.

Micro-interactions and other design details

On Dribbble I collect smaller visual and motion design details or ideas that would otherwise get buried in a case study.
