Ernst & Prosper?

This page outlines why I'm interested in joining the Prosper design team, and highlights some of my previous work.

About me

I'm Ernst, a Dutch designer based in New York (LI). Over the past 11 years, I've worked in mostly in product, user experience, and interaction design, but also in branding, motion design and animation. I've spent the majority of my time in-house at startups.

I've explored working for myself, in agencies, and big tech in China, and I've found I'm happiest when I work on the product side of design, in highly collaborative organizations.

I currently lead design for Our World in Data, an online publication and set of data visualization tools that makes research and data on the world's biggest problems accessible, understandable, and usable.

My current role as the only designer/product manager includes user research, scoping features, lo-fi and hi-fi design and prototyping, usability testing, shaping the roadmap, guiding long-term strategy, and evaluating product performance.

I work with a great team of smart, dedicated, and thoughtful people, and the work is meaningful. But what I’m missing, and what I think Prosper could offer, is a team with more design peers and veterans.

A team in which I could spread myself a little less thin, while still having a fair amount of product and strategic impact through design. A place where I could learn from, collaborate with, and help other designers.

Previous work

Decreasing the distance to data

The first stage of moving a few key numbers to the top of the Our World in Data covid-19 vaccinations page. User research findings and a quick comparison with Google's search results page made the problem clear and the first step towards a solution straightforward, and data about returning users showed that the timing was right to execute on a related idea.

AQI.fyi

A personal project where I explore if and how much the air quality in Beijing is improving. The weather in Beijing is quite predictable. It's the air quality that determines whether it's a nice day to be outside. There are good and bad months throughout the year, but it's hard to tell if things are actually improving or not without looking at the data. (work in progress, best viewed on desktop)

Users and change—the Δ in design

A case study about how the “projects” feature at Top20Talent evolved over the course of ~1.5 years. There's a good amount of detail in there. Looking back, I have some doubts about the direction we took towards the end.

The horror of usability testing

A piece I wrote about how designers should take a page from the horror movie genre. Observing a usability test is remarkably similar to watching a horror movie: it’s a chance for things to take a turn, from the safety of the ‘lab’.

Micro-interactions and other design details

On Dribbble I collect smaller visual and motion design details, and ideas that would otherwise get buried in a case study.

Beating gravity

NHLO created a fantastic technology that makes objects virtually weightless. I worked on a short explainer animation to give this claim the necessary weight.
